# Reasoning Task Evaluation Guide

## What to Look For

- Clear identification of premises and assumptions
- Logical chain from premises to conclusion
- Consideration of alternative viewpoints or edge cases
- Proper use of deductive, inductive, or abductive reasoning

## Common Failure Patterns

- **Logic Error**: Invalid inference steps, non-sequiturs, or circular reasoning
- **Incomplete Answer**: Reaching a conclusion without showing intermediate steps
- **Instruction Miss**: Answering a different question than what was asked
- **Hallucination**: Introducing facts not present in the given information

## Scoring Guidelines

### Instruction Following
- 5: Addresses every aspect of the reasoning prompt including constraints
- 3: Addresses the main question but misses secondary requirements
- 1: Answers a completely different question

### Reasoning Quality
- 5: Flawless logical chain with explicit step-by-step reasoning
- 3: Correct conclusion but with gaps in the reasoning chain
- 1: Fundamentally flawed logic or no reasoning shown

### Completeness
- 5: All premises examined, edge cases considered, conclusion well-supported
- 3: Main argument made but missing nuances
- 1: Only a partial or superficial attempt

### Clarity
- 5: Each step clearly labeled and easy to follow
- 3: Understandable but could be better structured
- 1: Confusing or contradictory presentation
