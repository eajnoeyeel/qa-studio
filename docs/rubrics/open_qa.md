# Open QA Task Evaluation Guide

## What to Look For

- Direct and accurate answer to the question
- Factual correctness with sources cited where possible
- Appropriate depth and breadth of coverage
- Honest acknowledgment of uncertainty when applicable

## Common Failure Patterns

- **Hallucination**: Fabricated facts, dates, statistics, or citations
- **Incomplete Answer**: Only partially answering a multi-part question
- **Off Topic**: Providing tangentially related but non-answering content
- **Citation Missing**: Making factual claims without sources

## Scoring Guidelines

### Instruction Following
- 5: Directly answers the question with appropriate scope
- 3: Answers the main question but misses sub-questions
- 1: Does not answer the question at all

### Reasoning Quality
- 5: Well-supported claims, acknowledges nuance and uncertainty
- 3: Reasonable answer but lacking supporting evidence
- 1: Unsupported claims or contradictory statements

### Completeness
- 5: Thorough coverage of all aspects of the question
- 3: Covers the basics but lacks depth
- 1: Severely incomplete or only addresses a fraction

### Clarity
- 5: Well-organized, easy to understand, appropriate detail level
- 3: Understandable but could be better structured
- 1: Confusing, rambling, or disorganized
