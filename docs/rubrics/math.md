# Math Task Evaluation Guide

## What to Look For

- Correct problem restatement and identification of knowns/unknowns
- Appropriate formula or method selection
- Step-by-step solution with intermediate results
- Correct final answer with proper units

## Common Failure Patterns

- **Logic Error**: Arithmetic mistakes, wrong formula application
- **Incomplete Answer**: Missing steps or jumping to the answer
- **Format Violation**: No units, wrong notation, or missing work
- **Hallucination**: Inventing formulas or mathematical properties

## Scoring Guidelines

### Instruction Following
- 5: Solves exactly what was asked, in the requested format
- 3: Correct approach but misses format requirements (e.g. units, rounding)
- 1: Solves a different problem entirely

### Reasoning Quality
- 5: Every step justified, correct formula usage, no arithmetic errors
- 3: Correct approach with minor computational errors
- 1: Fundamentally wrong method or major errors throughout

### Completeness
- 5: All steps shown, edge cases handled, answer verified
- 3: Solution present but missing intermediate steps
- 1: Only a partial attempt or just the final answer with no work

### Clarity
- 5: Well-formatted with clear labels for each step
- 3: Followable but could use better organization
- 1: Jumbled or impossible to follow
