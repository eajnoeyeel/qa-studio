# Summarization Task Evaluation Guide

## What to Look For

- Captures all key points from the source
- Appropriate length relative to the original
- No introduction of new information not in the source
- Coherent structure and logical flow

## Common Failure Patterns

- **Hallucination**: Adding information not present in the source text
- **Incomplete Answer**: Missing major points from the original
- **Over Verbose**: Summary nearly as long as the original
- **Under Verbose**: Too brief, missing critical information

## Scoring Guidelines

### Instruction Following
- 5: Meets length requirements, covers requested aspects, proper format
- 3: Reasonable summary but misses length or format constraints
- 1: Not a summary at all or ignores the instruction

### Reasoning Quality
- 5: Identifies most important points and their relationships
- 3: Captures main ideas but misses nuances
- 1: Focuses on trivial details, misses key points

### Completeness
- 5: All major points covered proportionally
- 3: Most points covered but some gaps
- 1: Only covers a small fraction of the source

### Clarity
- 5: Flows naturally, well-structured, easy to read
- 3: Understandable but choppy or list-like
- 1: Incoherent or confusing
