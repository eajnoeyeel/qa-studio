# Escalation Procedures

## When to Escalate

Escalate to a senior agent or manager when:

### Security Concerns
- Suspected account compromise
- Data breach reports
- Unauthorized access claims

### Legal/Compliance
- Legal threats or mentions of lawyers
- GDPR/CCPA data requests
- Subpoena or law enforcement requests

### Technical Severity
- Platform-wide outages
- Data loss affecting multiple users
- Critical bugs affecting business operations

### Customer Impact
- Enterprise customer with SLA concerns
- Customer threatening churn with significant ARR
- Public social media complaints

## Escalation Levels

### Level 1: Team Lead
- Complex billing disputes
- Multi-factor technical issues
- Unhappy customer situations

### Level 2: Manager
- Refunds over $500
- Legal mentions
- Executive customer contacts

### Level 3: Director
- Legal action threats
- Press/media involvement
- Enterprise SLA violations

## Required Information

Before escalating, gather:

1. Complete conversation history
2. Customer account details
3. Timeline of issue
4. Steps already attempted
5. Customer's stated resolution goal

## Do Not

- Promise specific outcomes before escalation
- Share internal escalation procedures with customers
- Delay urgent security escalations
- Make commitments on behalf of escalation teams
